## Objective
To analyze how market sentiment (Fear/Greed) influences crypto trader performance and behavior using:
- Historical trader data from Hyperliquid
- Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index data

## Key Findings

### Profitability
- Traders had **higher average profits during Greed** days.

### Trade Volume
- **More trades occurred on Fear days**, but with lower average returns.

### Coin-Level Insight
- Coins like **BTC** and **ETH** performed better during **Greed** periods.

## Visuals
- Boxplot of `Closed PnL` by sentiment
- Countplot of number of trades by sentiment
- *(Optional)* Heatmap of average coin performance vs sentiment

## Tools & Libraries Used
- `pandas`, `numpy`, `matplotlib`, `seaborn`
- Jupyter Notebook for data exploration

## Conclusion
Market sentiment significantly impacts trader behavior:
- Greed leads to more profitable but selective trading
- Fear triggers frequent trades with lower returns

These patterns can be leveraged to develop sentiment-aware trading strategies in crypto markets.
